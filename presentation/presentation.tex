  \item \textbf{Ridge}---linear baseline for comparison
  \item Each $\times$ \textbf{PTO-adjusted} variant (6 total)
\end{itemize}

\vspace{3pt}
{\small\textbf{\color{AgedGold}Predict-then-Optimize (PTO):}}\\
{\scriptsize Shifts predictions by newsvendor critical ratio:\\
$q^* = \hat{D} \cdot (1 + (CR - 0.5) \cdot \text{strength})$\\
Intentionally biases toward the cost-optimal quantile.}

\vspace{3pt}
{\small\textbf{\color{AgedGold}Features:}}\\
{\scriptsize Auto-engineered: label-encoded categoricals, year trend, season. Agent adds domain features via code.}

\vspace{3pt}
{\small\textbf{\color{AgedGold}Output Per SKU:}}\\
{\scriptsize Point estimate, P10/P50/P90 bounds, feature importance, decision cost}

\column{0.45\textwidth}
\begin{alertblock}{Why Newsvendor Cost, Not RMSE?}
\scriptsize
Two models with \emph{identical RMSE} can yield very different ordering costs.\\[3pt]
The newsvendor penalizes errors \textbf{asymmetrically}: under-prediction (stockout) costs up to \textbf{5$\times$} more than over-prediction.\\[3pt]
$\Rightarrow$ We rank models by the \textbf{realized newsvendor cost} of the orders they imply.\\[3pt]
{\tiny Following Elmachtoub \& Grigas (2022)}
\end{alertblock}

\vspace{3pt}
\begin{block}{Tuning Levers}
\scriptsize
Agent can adjust: tree depth, learning rate, estimator count, PTO strength, train/test year split
\end{block}
\end{columns}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Optimization Engine}
\goldrule
\vspace{2pt}

{\small\textbf{\color{AgedGold}Formulation:} Sample Average Approximation (SAA) of the multi-product newsvendor}

\vspace{1pt}
{\small$$\min_{\mathbf{q}} \; \sum_{i=1}^{N} \left[ c_i q_i + \frac{1}{S}\sum_{s=1}^{S} \left( o_i (q_i - d_{is})^+ + u_i (d_{is} - q_i)^+ \right) \right]$$}

\vspace{-2pt}
\begin{columns}[T]
\column{0.48\textwidth}
{\small\textbf{\color{AgedGold}Five Solvers Benchmarked:}}
\begin{enumerate}[label={\color{AgedGold}\bfseries\arabic*.}]\scriptsize
  \item \textbf{SLSQP}---sequential quadratic programming
  \item \textbf{L-BFGS-B}---quasi-Newton, box-constrained
  \item \textbf{PGD}---projected gradient descent
  \item \textbf{SGD}---stochastic gradient descent
  \item \textbf{Adam}---adaptive moment estimation
\end{enumerate}
{\tiny Multiple random restarts to handle non-convexity.}

\column{0.48\textwidth}
{\small\textbf{\color{AgedGold}Constraint Handling:}}
\begin{itemize}\scriptsize
  \item Budget: hard constraint via SLSQP; projection for first-order
  \item MOQ: floor projection ($q_i \geq 6$)
  \item Non-negativity: box bounds
\end{itemize}

\vspace{3pt}
{\small\textbf{\color{AgedGold}Agent Verification:}}
\begin{itemize}\scriptsize
  \item All solvers feasible? Budget binding or slack?
  \item Realized cost vs.\ expected cost
  \item Agent plan vs.\ naive baseline
\end{itemize}
\end{columns}
\end{frame}

%% TWO-PERIOD JOINT
%% ====================================================================
\begin{frame}{Two-Period Joint Optimization (Model B)}
\goldrule
\vspace{2pt}
\begin{columns}[T]
\column{0.55\textwidth}
{\small\textbf{\color{AgedGold}The Jersey Lifecycle Problem:}}\\
{\scriptsize Adidas models (e.g., Tiro~25) produced for ${\sim}$2 years. Year~2 stockouts mean sourcing discontinued jerseys at \textbf{5$\times$ cost} or losing sales.}

\vspace{3pt}
{\small\textbf{\color{AgedGold}Key Insight:}}\\
{\scriptsize Year~1 surplus carries to Year~2 at near-zero cost---a \textbf{free hedge} against Year~2's extreme underage penalty.}

\vspace{3pt}
{\small\textbf{\color{AgedGold}Joint Formulation:}}
{\small\begin{align*}
\min_{q_1, q_2} \; & \mathbb{E}\Bigl[\textstyle\sum_s \bigl( u_1 [D_1 - q_1]^+ + o_2 [q_2 + I_s - D_2]^+ \\[-2pt]
  & \quad + u_2 [D_2 - q_2 - I_s]^+ \bigr)\Bigr]
\end{align*}}%
\vspace{-8pt}
{\scriptsize where $I_s = \max(q_1 - D_1, 0)$ = carryover inventory}

\column{0.42\textwidth}
\begin{block}{Model A vs.\ Model B}
\centering\scriptsize
\begin{tabular}{@{}l c c@{}}
\toprule
& \textbf{A} & \textbf{B} \\
\midrule
Scope     & Independent & Joint \\
Periods   & 1 at a time & Both linked \\
Carryover & Ignored     & Modeled \\
Y2 hedge  & None        & Built-in \\
Solver    & Per-period  & SAA joint \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{block}

\vspace{3pt}
\begin{exampleblock}{Why Joint Matters}
\scriptsize
Independent solving \emph{misses} the carryover hedge. Joint model can deliberately over-order in Year~1 (cheap overage) to reduce Year~2 risk (expensive underage).\\[2pt]
$\Rightarrow$ ``Cost of myopia'' quantified
\end{exampleblock}
\end{columns}
\end{frame}

%% ====================================================================
%% MANAGERIAL TAKEAWAYS
%% ====================================================================
\begin{frame}{Managerial Takeaways}
\goldrule
\vspace{4pt}
\begin{enumerate}[label={\color{AgedGold}\bfseries\arabic*.}, itemsep=5pt]\small
  \item \textbf{The agent produces a budget-feasible, cost-minimizing order plan}---something the current ``gut feel'' process cannot guarantee.

  \item \textbf{Focus on youth jerseys}---they drive the majority of spend and forecasting risk. Adult/women sizes are often set by MOQ.

  \item \textbf{The budget is the binding constraint}, not the forecast model. If the budget is too tight, no algorithm can prevent stockouts.

  \item \textbf{Cost estimates matter more than demand accuracy}---the plan is sensitive to underage cost assumptions and nearly flat to overage.

  \item \textbf{Re-run when conditions change}: new cost data, updated budget, new season of orders, jersey model transition.

  \item \textbf{The system is generic}---the same pipeline can optimize any newsvendor-class inventory problem, not just soccer uniforms.
\end{enumerate}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Limitations \& Future Work}
\goldrule
\vspace{2pt}
\begin{columns}[T]
\column{0.48\textwidth}
{\small\textbf{\color{AccentRed}Current Limitations:}}
\begin{itemize}\scriptsize
  \item Size-level demand inherently noisy---tail spikes not predictable from history alone
  \item Sparse data for rare sizes (AXL, WXL)---MOQ dominates
  \item Pipeline runtime ${\sim}$10 min (optimizer-dominated)
  \item LLM output quality varies between runs
\end{itemize}

\column{0.48\textwidth}
{\small\textbf{\color{AccentGreen}Future Improvements:}}
\begin{itemize}\scriptsize
  \item \textbf{Registration/roster data} as a leading demand indicator
  \item \textbf{Hierarchical models} to borrow strength across sizes
  \item \textbf{Carryover-net ordering} as a first-class constraint
  \item \textbf{End-to-end prescriptive model} on newsvendor loss
  \item Interactive config editor in web UI
\end{itemize}
